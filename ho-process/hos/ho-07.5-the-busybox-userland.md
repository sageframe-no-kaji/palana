# ho-07.5 ‚Äî The BusyBox Userland

Inserted when zencat answered: the probe classified its BusyBox as BSD, the BSD listing command asked for a `stat` binary the box does not carry, and usage spew surfaced as a typed read failure. The practitioner's firmware box deserves better than a riddle. This ho gives the probe a third honest answer and the Listing a path that speaks what BusyBox actually has.

The recon facts, read live from zencat (BusyBox v1.25.1, vendor-trimmed 2025): no `stat` binary at all. `find` carries only `-name`, `-mtime`, `-print0`, `-exec` ‚Äî no `-mindepth`, `-maxdepth`, `-type`. `sh` lacks the `command` builtin. It HAS `awk`, and `ls -lane` works.

**Out of scope:** rsync/zfs anything on BusyBox hosts ‚Äî the probe records their absence and ho-08's fact gates already route to tar. Recursive size facts on BusyBox ‚Äî no walkable `find`, so plans show the inode floor with `totalSizeComplete == false`, the honesty ho-06.5 built. Mutating verbs beyond what the engine already composes (BusyBox has `mv`, `cp`, `rm`, `tar`).

## Phase 1 ‚Äî Think

### Decision 1 ‚Äî A third flavor, told by the busybox binary itself

`UserlandFlavor` grows `.busybox`. The probe's tell, in order: `stat --version` answers ‚Üí GNU; else `busybox true` exits 0 ‚Üí BusyBox; else BSD. A GNU host with busybox installed stays GNU (first tell wins); a Mac has no busybox and stays BSD. Old cached facts stay decodable ‚Äî the new case is additive.

### Decision 2 ‚Äî The listing is `ls -lane`, degradation named, never guessed

The choice was degraded fidelity versus refusal, and a browseable firmware box beats a locked one ‚Äî but every degradation is named, in the parser's contract and the field docs: owner and group are numeric (`-n`), the mtime is `ls`'s own clock text with second precision at best and the remote's timezone unstated, and a name that breaks the line structure (a newline) refuses the whole listing loudly ‚Äî `malformedListing`, never a silently wrong row. Kinds come from the mode string's first column; symlink targets from the ` -> ` split. `.` and `..` drop.

### Decision 3 ‚Äî What BusyBox cannot do stays visible, not special-cased

`treeSizes` on `.busybox` returns no facts ‚Äî no `-type f`, no walk ‚Äî and the existing floor machinery does the honest work: plans over BusyBox directories show the inode floor and say it is one. The transports need nothing: ho-08's rsync gate already falls to tar when rsync is absent, and BusyBox tar unpacks a stream fine.

### Decision 4 ‚Äî The stale-fact edge is ho-09's, named here

zencat's cache may still say BSD from the misclassified visit. The cache is deletable memory by design; the explicit re-probe control is the field view's (ho-09). Until then: delete `field-cache.json` or point at a fresh host name.

### Discovery (deferred to execution) ‚Äî the fixture's userland

Whether a plain-Alpine sshd container (BusyBox userland, no coreutils) stands up cheaply beside the existing fixture. If it does, integration runs live; if not, the recon shapes and synthetic battery carry, and the fixture rides a follow-up.
